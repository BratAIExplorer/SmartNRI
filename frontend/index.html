Malaysia EP Salaries Double from June 2026

The Ministry of Home Affairs has confirmed dramatically higher minimum salary thresholds for all Employment Pass categories, effective for new applications and renewals from 1 June 2026.

What Changed

Category I minimum jumps from RM10,000 to RM20,000/month
Category II now RM10,000–RM19,999 (was RM5,000–RM9,999)
Category III now RM5,000–RM9,999 with a mandatory 5-year cap
Succession planning now mandatory for Category II & III holders
Source: ESD/MOHA Official

India Budget 2026: Key Changes Every NRI Must Know

The new Income Tax Act 2025 takes effect 1 April 2026. Major simplifications for NRI compliance, plus a one-time foreign asset disclosure window that closes soon.

What Matters to You

TCS on overseas tour packages reduced from 5% to 2%

Property TDS simplified — buyers use PAN instead of TAN (from Oct 2026)

17 Feb 2026

Still Using a Resident Savings Account? You’re at Risk.

Under FEMA, NRIs cannot hold resident savings accounts. Penalties can reach ₹2 lakh + ₹5,000/day for continued violations. Banks are increasing scrutiny in 2026.

Action Required

Convert resident savings accounts to NRO immediately upon becoming NRI
Open NRE account for foreign earnings (tax-free interest, fully repatriable)
NRO handles Indian income (rent, dividends) — taxable, USD 1M/yr repatriation cap
Penalty: up to 3× the account balance or ₹2 lakh, whichever is higher
Source: RBI Master Circular

The 120-Day Trap: How a Long India Visit Could Cost You Lakhs

Earn more than ₹15 lakh from Indian sources and spend over 120 days in India? You may become a “Deemed Resident” — making your global income taxable in India.

Know the Rules

120-day rule applies only if Indian-sourced income exceeds ₹15 lakh/year
Malaysia exempts foreign income until 2036 — but India’s rules are independent
The India–Malaysia DTAA can protect you, but only if you plan ahead and file correctly
Source: Income Tax Act, Section 6
